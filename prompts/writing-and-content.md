# Writing & Content Prompts

A curated pack of prompts for stripping the "AI tell" from writing and for building
voice and hooks for content. Copy a prompt, replace the `[BRACKETED]` placeholders, and
run it in Claude or ChatGPT.

Two sections:
- **Humanise AI writing** — five rewrite passes that break rigid structure and rehearsed phrasing.
- **Content voice & hooks** — discover your voice, generate hooks, match your voice, and add an identity line.

> Tip: the humanise prompts work well stacked. Run the Real Person Rewrite first, then
> apply one or two of the others to the same text.

---

## Humanise AI writing

### 1. Real Person Rewrite
**When to use:** First pass on any AI draft — turns "constructed" prose into something that sounds spoken.

```
You are an editor with a sharp eye for writing that reads as constructed rather than
spoken. Rewrite the text below the way someone who actually lived it would say it:
a little rougher, more direct. Cut anything that feels rehearsed, overbuilt, or
written to sound impressive.

Text: [PASTE YOUR TEXT]
```

### 2. Thought Flow Fix
**When to use:** When a draft is technically fine but reads too smooth and evenly paced.

```
This is not a grammar fix. It is a thinking fix. Rewrite the text below so the ideas
move the way a real mind moves: uneven in places, punchy in others, sometimes slower.
Break any pattern where the writing feels too controlled or too evenly paced.

Text: [PASTE YOUR TEXT]
```

### 3. Pattern Disruptor
**When to use:** When you want the specific AI "tells" hunted down and removed one by one.

```
You specialize in spotting the tells that expose AI writing. Go through the text below
and strip them out one by one. Vary the structure, soften rigid phrasing, and make each
word feel chosen in the moment rather than generated by a pattern.

Text: [PASTE YOUR TEXT]
```

### 4. Credibility Test
**When to use:** When the writing feels too clean or too precise to be trusted by a reader.

```
You are a reader who immediately distrusts writing that sounds too clean. Go sentence by
sentence through the text below and rewrite anything that feels constructed, overcorrected,
or too precise. It should read like something a real person said out loud.

Text: [PASTE YOUR TEXT]
```

### 5. Voice Shaper
**When to use:** When a draft reads like a neutral observer and needs a clear point of view.

```
You are building a voice that sounds like it belongs to one real person. Rewrite the text
below so it carries a clear point of view, not the tone of a neutral observer trying to
please everyone. Let opinions, small contradictions, and natural shifts in tone come through.

Text: [PASTE YOUR TEXT]
```

## Content voice & hooks

### 1. Voice discovery (before writing)
**When to use:** Run this once at the start of a session so the model studies you before it writes anything.

```
I'm going to tell you my story. Don't write anything yet. Just listen, then ask me 5
questions to understand my brand voice, my audience, and what makes me different.

My story: [DESCRIBE YOUR BACKGROUND, WHAT YOU DO, AND WHO YOU SERVE]
```

*Why it works: the model studies you before it writes, so the output stops sounding generic.*

### 2. Hook generator
**When to use:** When you need a batch of scroll-stopping openers for a reel, post, or video.

```
Give me 10 hook options for a [reel / post / video] about [TOPIC].

Use these formats:
- Personal confession
- Controversial opinion
- Specific number
- Before and after

Make them sound like I'm texting a friend.
```

*Pick the one that stops your own scroll.*

### 3. Voice match
**When to use:** Final rewrite pass to force a draft into your plain, human voice.

```
Rewrite the text below in my voice. Short sentences. Around a third-grade reading level.
No corporate words. No em dashes. Sound like a real person, not a guru.

Text: [PASTE YOUR TEXT]
```

### 4. Identity layer
**When to use:** When a post explains what you offer but never establishes who you are.

```
Add one line to the text below that tells my audience WHO I am before I tell them WHAT I do.
Reference my origin story without sounding braggy.

My origin story: [ONE OR TWO SENTENCES ON WHERE YOU STARTED]
Text: [PASTE YOUR TEXT]
```
